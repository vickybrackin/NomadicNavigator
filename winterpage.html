Morzine, France

Part of the Portes du Soleil ski area with over 650 kilometres of skiable area spread across 12 resorts in France and Switzerland, Morzine is a great option for all levels of ability. It has a picturesque town centre with plenty of restaurants and lively bars but also many activities for families and non-skiers. A 90-minute journey from Geneva airport makes this a perfect destination for shorter breaks.




St. Anton am Arlberg, Austria

Known for its extremely vibrant Apres-Ski scene, chocolate box style village centre and advanced ski terrain, St Anton is the perfect pick for those wanting a challenge on the slopes followed by a night out in one of the town's many lively bars or clubs. The skiable area is large with real gems to be found off-piste for those feeling adventurous.




La Plagne, France

Perfect for families with lots of terrain to suit all abilities and many choices for accommodation across the resort. Linked with Les Arcs as part of the larger Paradiski area, there is plenty to keep the entire group entertained.




Cervinia, Italy

The highest resort in Italy at 2000 metres, Cervinia boasts snow-sure skiing with access to neighboring Zermatt and views of the Matterhorn mountain. Excellent local restaurants mean some great food options for all tastes.
